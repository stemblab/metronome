Notes on modelling a metronome (under construction)

A metronome may be modelled [http://www.uaa.alaska.edu/Physicsandastronomy/upload/metro.pdf, (pdf)] as a combination of [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pendulum, pendulum] and [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_der_Pol_oscillator, Van der Pol oscillator]. In a [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pendulum#Compound_pendulum, compound pendulum] the equivalent length is $$ L = \frac{I}{MR} $$ where $I$ is the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moment_of_inertia, moment of inertia], $M$ is the total mass, and $R$ is the distance from the pivot to the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_of_mass, center of mass]. For two point masses $$ \begin{align} I &= m_1 r_1^2 + m_2 r_2^2 \nonumber \\ R &= \frac{m_1 x_1 + m_2 x_2}{m_1+m_2} \nonumber \end{align} $$
$$ \large \ddot{\theta}+\frac{g}{l}\sin\theta = \varepsilon \left(1-\frac{\theta^2}{\theta_0^2}\right)\dot{\theta} $$
